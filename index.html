600-1,000 Word Essay/Post

Brief Description: This piece is an example of a typical 600-1,000 word essay or post. It is important when writing such posts to be clear and concise about your topic, to use facts to back up your argument, but to use language that is easily understandable for the audience.

Open Letter

Brief Description: A major project at MTA impacted hundreds of thousands of residents in Baltimore City. Brainstorming ideas to reach the public and spread awareness of the project, I constructed a letter from our CEO that could be posted as a full-page advertisement in our major newspaper. Larger companies in Baltimore, including Kevin Plank's Under Armour, had used a similar strategy when trying to reach out to residents. This letter contained an exciting description and argument for the project as well as details of the that residents needed to be aware of.

Correspondence

Brief Description: For initiatives that impact customers, there is bound to be an influx of questions and concerns. The approach I took with correspondence such as this example, was to state that I understood where they were coming from, address their concern with the data available, and thank them for reaching out. In addition to the dozens of correspondence letters I wrote on behalf of the MTA, I also wrote constituent letters and emails on behalf of a state senator while working for him in 2011. Great customer service makes the customer feel appreciated and reflects well on the entire agency.

Public Response

Brief Description: While working for the Maryland Transit Administration, we would receive correspondence from community and advocacy organizations and respond to each and every one. This particular response was sensitive as the organization was making their claims public and advocating against our agency. My response, on behalf of the CEO, maintains a cordial and professional tone while also stressing our position and is stern in pointing out flaws in their argument.

Press Release

Brief Description: During my time in the Maryland State Senate, the legislator that I worked for put together an aggressive and controversial package of bills that often required explanation. I produced press releases on his legislative initiatives that were clear and concise, interesting news-worthy topics, and invited the press and organizations to reach out for more information. During my time at both Compass Marketing Inc and Maryland Transit Administration, I would produce content and edit press releases for accuracy on behalf of the communications liaison.

Talking Points

Brief Description: When a major government initiative launches, the agency is bound to get bombarded with questions from the press and public. I wanted to make sure that our executives were prepared to answer tough questions and had all the information available to them. Our project impacted hundreds of thousands of residents in the Baltimore region and over 18 months, we had an aggressive outreach campaign to spread awareness about the changes. These talking points relayed the importance of public outreach and education and broke down the exact components of our campaign, proving that no avenue was overlooked.
